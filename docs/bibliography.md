# Bibliography

Primary sources cited across the atlas. Each dossier also carries its own references
block; this page is the rolled-up master list. Prefer these over citing the atlas itself.

## Climate — Integrated Assessment

### DICE / RICE (Nordhaus, Yale)

- Nordhaus, W. D. (1992). *An Optimal Transition Path for Controlling Greenhouse Gases.*
  **Science**, 258(5086), 1315–1319.
- Nordhaus, W. D. (1994). *Managing the Global Commons: The Economics of Climate Change.*
  MIT Press.
- Nordhaus, W. D., & Boyer, J. (2000). *Warming the World: Economic Models of Global
  Warming.* MIT Press. (RICE)
- Nordhaus, W. D. (2008). *A Question of Balance: Weighing the Options on Global Warming
  Policies.* Yale University Press.
- Nordhaus, W. D. (2017). *Revisiting the Social Cost of Carbon.* **PNAS**, 114(7),
  1518–1523.
- Nordhaus, W. D. (2018). *Climate Change: The Ultimate Challenge for Economics.*
  Nobel Prize Lecture / **American Economic Review** 109(6).

### Critiques & the discounting / uncertainty debate

- Pindyck, R. S. (2013). *Climate Change Policy: What Do the Models Tell Us?*
  **Journal of Economic Literature**, 51(3), 860–872.
- Stern, N. (2007). *The Economics of Climate Change: The Stern Review.* Cambridge UP.
- Weitzman, M. L. (2009). *On Modeling and Interpreting the Economics of Catastrophic
  Climate Change* ("Dismal Theorem"). **Review of Economics and Statistics**, 91(1).
- Cai, Y., & Judd, K. L. (2019). *Advances in numerical dynamic programming and new
  applications* / DSICE work on stochastic DICE.

### Policy use

- Interagency Working Group on Social Cost of Greenhouse Gases (U.S.), *Technical
  Support Documents* (2010, 2016, 2021) — DICE, FUND, PAGE.
- U.S. EPA (2022). *Report on the Social Cost of Greenhouse Gases* — GIVE model.
- IPCC, *Sixth Assessment Report*, Working Group III (2022).

## Method & foundations

- Ramsey, F. P. (1928). *A Mathematical Theory of Saving.* **Economic Journal**, 38(152).
